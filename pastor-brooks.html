Founder & CEO

Pastor Corey B. Brooks

The “Rooftop Pastor.” Founder and CEO of Project H.O.O.D., senior pastor of New Beginnings Church of Chicago, and a relentless voice for Chicago’s South Side.

In 2011, Pastor Brooks moved onto the rooftop of an abandoned motel across from a funeral parlor and stayed 94 nights — through a Chicago winter — to protest the youth gun deaths tearing through Woodlawn. Project H.O.O.D. was born soon after.

More than a decade later, he leads an organization that serves 15,000+ people a year and is building the $45M Leadership & Economic Opportunity (LEO) Center. In 2025 he walked 900+ miles from Chicago to New York to help fund it.

The story

How we got here.

2012 · Founded

Pastor Brooks takes a stand against neighborhood violence by spending 94 nights on a motel rooftop across from a funeral parlor to protest youth gun deaths. Project H.O.O.D. launches soon after.

2015–2020 · Programs scale

Workforce development partnerships grow from dozens to hundreds of placements. The violence-interruption team formalizes across Woodlawn.

2022–2024 · LEO breaks ground

The capital campaign launches for the Leadership and Economic Opportunity Center — a 90,000 sq ft hub on S. King Drive. Groundbreaking in 2022.

2025 · Walk Across America

Pastor Brooks walks 900+ miles from Chicago to New York to raise the final funds for LEO and spotlight what’s possible. 15,000+ served this year.
